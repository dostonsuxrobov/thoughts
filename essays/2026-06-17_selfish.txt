it sounds selfish when someone says it, but at its core, a human has limited energy. to make changes in atoms, you actually need a lot of it, even in local forms.

thinking about responsibilities to evolution, Earth, your country, your parents, animals, or holy spirits makes everything more complicated and leaves a lot of room for open-ended questions where energy leaks indefinitely.

thinking about a deer being eaten by a crocodile or a poor child somewhere in the world is often a waste of energy—not because caring is evil, but because you cannot rearrange those atoms in the first place.

individual life, in its local form, is already complex enough, let alone global events.

to make things work, one needs energy. atoms do not rearrange themselves into order; they quickly face entropy. it takes a great deal of energy to create and maintain order, and doing so globally is impossibly hard simply because of a lack of energy. you simply do not have enough joules for it.

the best approach, i think, is to ignore what you cannot affect. it is important for the brain to close loops; open loops can consume attention indefinitely. in that sense, ignorance is useful.

simply: ignore the things you cannot control and focus where your efforts matter. energy is a finite resource.

simpler: delete → focus.
