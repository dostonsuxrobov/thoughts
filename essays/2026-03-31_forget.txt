forgetfulness prevents two things at the same time on fundamental levels: hubris and misery.

misery? i believe that misery does not come from external circumstances; it comes from excess layers of memories that keep charging rent. it happens when the brain has to process through massive layers of abstraction to make very simple decisions. in such moments, a person feels stuck, agency feels locked, and life feels impossible, as every move seems to be stacked against them. in reality, most misery is self-constructed, and primarily it exists in the mind itself. when you refuse to let go of memories and keep counting your own or someone else’s mistakes or any “unfairness,” it piles up like garbage in your mind, making rational thinking nearly impossible. this can even result in depression, as a person becomes a prisoner of their own mind.

hubris? the reverse is also true. when someone succeeds, they often forget that it was the correct application of physics, timing, or one of thousands of factors that helped them achieve a win. instead, they start thinking they are special. this “cookie jar” effect makes them think they are a god. this is a critical failure mode, as they no longer see themselves as part of an ecosystem but rather as some supernatural being with a higher purpose above others. this leads to very irrational decision-making and a fragile ego.

forgetting is an antidote for both. it removes the old and gives way to the new. you are no longer tied back by your past, whether good or bad. you learn, delegate information to external sources, and keep moving forward. it frees limited resources to be used for things that matter.

life always introduces problems that one needs to face. increasing mental load rarely helps in finding critical solutions. the best approach is to take what you have, look at what is in front of you, find the critical focal points of the issue, understand your own levers, apply maximum freed-up energy, break the issue, and keep moving forward.

simpler: forget → find critical point → concentrate → repeat

life does not need to be hard.
