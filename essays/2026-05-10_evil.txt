life is a resource management task.

the idea of evil suggests that some entities are uniquely focused on harming others, but at its core, that idea is bullshit. every living thing, and even machines, are essentially trying to balance resources in order to exist. the primary programmed objective of living beings is to survive as long as possible.

almost all wars, hatred, or what people call “evil” comes down to one side wanting resources from another side while the other refuses to give them up. it is not just about land, money, or oil, but also a huge variety of things, such as the feeling of superiority gained by damaging another person’s ego. at its core, it can be seen as a kind of ego transfer, but even that is less about evil itself and more about filling a psychological void by taking peace away from another person.

another form of so-called “evil” is entitlement. at its core, entitlement is the constant demand for resources from others, which can create many problems. 
for example, if you continuously help one side, they may become dependent on it. this creates a parasitic relationship where one side is drained while the other weakens through dependency. this applies not only to individuals but also to entire countries or empires. Margaret Thatcher is often used as an example here, as she argued something like “there is no free lunch anymore, so stand on your own feet,” a position that made her deeply controversial and widely hated, to the extend of trying to kill her.

the simplest way to deal with all of this is through detachment: saying “no” more often and focusing on what truly matters. everyone can stand on their own feet, and you should as well.

simply: there is no evil, not in animals, not in ai, and not in humans. there is only resource management, so do not hate anyone. instead, ignore what drains you and focus on your own anchors.

simpler: don’t hate -> simply don’t care.
