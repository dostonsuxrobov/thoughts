life is a resource management task, and stress mostly arises when your perceived scarcity is hitting its floor. basically, expectation minus reality determines stress or happiness.

for me personally, i assigned my own floor, which consists of 5 main components:
- oxygen
- water
- food
- sleep
- shelter, or any way to maintain thermal balance and avoid being too cold or too hot

i think a lot about how to delete it even further, but got diminishing returns while each component can be simplified: drinking simple water, eating simple food, simple way of sleeping, or having simple shelter can all be reduced to their most basic forms.

this is not about religion or philosophical narratives, but rather about rebuilding happiness from first principles with one question: "how can i be happy and useful?"

i think i found my own answers. happiness is not about a temporary spike in dopamine after sex, drugs, or sugar, but rather something stronger and more powerful. it is about meaning. it is about a lifelong anchor, something you can never fully achieve but that always serves as a point of alignment for your entire life.

that is why i wanted to mentally remove as much as possible from the category of "critical" in my life, so i could give absolute priority to the anchor.

the 5-part floor is basically a way to ensure that everything else has no meaning except as luxury. even if i am sleeping on the floor, this can help me be happy, even in prison or a dungeon, because the math is still serving me in positive signs.

it is simply a reduction of A in the equation p = F / A. if i need so little and i work hard, then i am useful. and if i am useful, i am happy, because i am already in abundance.

i assign my own meaning to my own life, and i choose to limit it to very few things so my brain can process it, my body can handle it, and my being can be . . . happy . . . i guess.

i do not want happiness that depends on fragile conditions or other people's moods.

i want happiness that emerges from alignment, usefulness, and a deliberately simplified definition of necessity.

i want to measure happiness by alignment, not stimulation.
i want to measure abundance by low dependency plus useful output.

simply : suffering = perceived deficit of "critical needs", happiness = stable alignment with chosen meaning, while the floor is protected.

simpler : simple needs -> stable meaning -> happiness
