working harder is stupid.

iterating harder is a force of nature.

when people talk about simply working harder, it mostly resembles a person banging their head against a wall again and again, hoping for a miracle or for others to notice and praise them. the truth is, no one cares, and it will simply burn that person out.

iteration is true power, especially when aligned with a strict cycle: question, delete, simplify, accelerate, and automate, the Musk algorithm. iterating harder through this cycle is true power, and it leads to real results. the reason has to do with the pure logic of reality itself. it is like, instead of banging your head against the wall, you try hundreds of ways around it: using a hammer, finding a weaker side, jumping over it, or maybe even changing your mind about the wall entirely.

working harder is ego-driven. you keep doing things just because they define your identity, and that identity becomes fragile. you accumulate things you do not need, and the laws of physics drag your progress down. you become rich but miserable, or poor and miserable.

iterating harder means you need to delete those notions, be ready to change your mind entirely, and make bets against your own previous judgments if they lead to tangible results, because reality is the ultimate judge, not your pride.

effort is linear, iteration is compounding.

simply: do not work harder. iterate harder by constantly cycling: question, delete, simplify, accelerate, and automate.

simpler: 1. delete -> 2. work.
