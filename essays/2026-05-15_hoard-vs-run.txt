life is a resource management task.

so how should we live? hoard or run?

hoarding is a way to outlive entropy. running is a way to outpace entropy.

in every way, entropy wins. guaranteed.

hoarding is the easiest path, but it also means you gain weight. you become heavy and hard to move forward. for centuries, humanity basically used it all the time, and it seems to be the default state.

how about running? i think it is uniquely harder while also being uniquely more interesting. 
here, the task is not to prepare for winter but to move fast enough to carry yourself from location to location. 
Mongols, especially under Genghis Khan, are probably the best example here: history’s second-biggest empire, with armies far ahead of their time.

holding on to anything, including memories, photos, things, items, animals, humans, hate, locations, or especially grudges, is not the best course of action. it is the worst, because even if you put food in a refrigerator, it still rots, rots slower, but it rots anyway.

imagine you have a choice: wait, hoard food, and prepare for the weather to change, or put some food in a backpack and run to a different location. i would choose the latter, even if it is riskier and harder, because it is also more meaningful.

my first choice would be to remove everything from the backpack, or not get a backpack at all, and run. run fast and long, because the backpack is not even yours anyway, and the food will rot soon.

nothing in life truly matters except life itself. it is not memories, people, or locations, but its own moments, pace, and most importantly, the feeling of it. you cannot take a photo of it or save it on your ssd. delete it all and run. feel the rush. feel the runner’s high. live like you are playing a game.

we actually don't need much : oxygen, water, food, sleep and some shelter. that's it.

simply: stay lean, delete, run, and enjoy the ride itself. don’t chase and don’t memorize. just run.

simpler: delete -> run
