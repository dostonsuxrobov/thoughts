life is a resource management task.

at the core, nothing belongs to us, not even ourselves, so treating the maximum number of variables as “critical” is a bad idea.

i believe misery is proportional to the number of things you hold in your mind.

misery = attachment load.

not external load. mental load.

it is about the mind, not anything else. you can have everything and still be miserable. the external world rarely changes the state of mind unless you give it root access. we are the only ones with admin access to it.

our lives are simulations created by the brain. it dictates the internal state of the organism and how life feels. the same event can be interpreted by two people in completely different ways. this does not make reality false, but rather highlights the brain’s simulation as the more prominent influence on how we actually feel.

humans are remarkably adaptable machines. i truly love being human, but we are also semi-fragile, not literally, but cognitively. we are remarkably resilient and possess bodies and brains capable of changing to remarkable degrees, while still being held back by ideas alone.

we own nothing, so trying to hold onto everything is a clear path to misery. let it go, all the time.

misery is never really about things, money, status, or people. it is about your own software, the simulation running inside you that dictates their weights and meaning.

the good news is that this software is largely under our control. we cannot change the weather or earth orbit, but we can completely change our minds.

simply: do not hold things in your mind. change your mind often. we never owned anything anyway.

simpler: let it go.
