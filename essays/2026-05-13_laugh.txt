life is a resource management task.

negativity is a resource sink; it drains a human without clear results.

positivity is the reverse; it enables the release of energy.

the human body already possesses an impressive energy capacity, far more than most people believe or can even comprehend, although this energy is not always readily available.

imagine it like a dam. the body constantly tries to prevent the use of energy for many reasons, and negativity is one of the strongest forces keeping that energy from being released. negative thinking itself can become the biggest and strongest dam of all.

positivity may sound naive, but it acts as an enabler that opens the gates and allows the only true currency of achievement to flow: energy.

i think the easiest way is to laugh, even without a reason, without clever jokes, or without anything happening at all. the body responds to patterns, and a smile is a powerful pattern that the body quickly recognizes. the rider and the elephant concept is not only about making others feel safe, but primarily about yourself. the happier the elephant believes things are, the more energy it allows you to use. you cannot reason with it directly; you can only guide it through patterns.

at its core, life is largely shaped by the simulations created by our brain. much of it is biochemistry driven by the elephant. if you are laughing, then the elephant assumes you are happy. it does not need to align with reality; it only needs to align with the elephant.

simply: laugh more and never hate. energy will become the byproduct.
