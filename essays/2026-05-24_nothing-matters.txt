"think of yourself as dead. you have lived your life. now, take what’s left and live it properly."
— Marcus Aurelius

i deeply love this quote . . . to the bone.

my version is simpler: "nothing matters."

the reason is to attack paralysis at its fundamental core, which is attachment.

time → attachment → fear of loss → paralysis

time creates attachments, attachments create fear of loss, fear creates paralysis. over time, this cycle accumulates so heavily that even the greatest emperors become prisoners of their own empires.

"nothing matters" is a way of deleting this very thing from its core and making things breathe again.

"nothing matters" is not just a trick. it is a fundamental truth. it is not only a mechanism to help you move again, but also a reminder that i am too small to matter, and life will continue with or without me. so let’s move, be useful, and be happy. i do not need to carry imagined burdens.

simply: delete attachment at its core. love, live, work, be usefull.

simpler: repeat, "nothing matters."
